Asphalt QC/QA workflow support

Materials & Process Support · Ohio Department of Transportation · 2025

This case covers my work supporting asphalt quality control and approval processes during the construction season at Ohio DOT.

The work wasn’t about building software or redesigning a system from scratch. It was about operating inside an existing, regulated process and helping it run more consistently under real pressure.

Context

I worked as an Asphalt Intern in the AASHTO-accredited Asphalt Materials Section. The section supports Ohio DOT projects by testing materials, reviewing mix designs, and approving asphalt used on active construction jobs.

The work directly supported project contract administration, supplier certification, and the state’s asphalt QC/QA program. Accuracy mattered. Mistakes showed up later in audits or in the field.

My role

I supported both the Asphalt Mix and Asphalt Binder areas during the construction season. Most days involved verifying materials and mix designs submitted for Ohio DOT projects.

  • Tested asphalt binders and emulsions from active construction projects
  • Recreated mix designs by mixing and compacting lab specimens
  • Checked moisture susceptibility and other required performance criteria
  • Reviewed new mix designs to confirm they met ODOT specifications
  • Helped benchmark mix designs as part of balanced mix design work

The job required careful documentation and consistency. Most issues weren’t dramatic. They were small mismatches that could slow approvals or create problems later if missed.

What made the work challenging

  • High volume during peak construction months
  • Strict specifications with little room for interpretation
  • Multiple stakeholders relying on timely approvals
  • Manual tracking across documents and spreadsheets

How I contributed

Beyond testing and review, I helped bring more structure to how issues were tracked and discussed during reviews.

  • Identified recurring issues across mix design submissions
  • Helped organize review notes so patterns were easier to spot
  • Supported clearer handoffs between review and approval steps

Impact

  • Fewer repeated clarification requests during reviews
  • More predictable turnaround on mix design approvals
  • Better preparation for audits and documentation checks

Tradeoffs

The work stayed intentionally conservative. Introducing new tools or processes would have added training overhead during the busiest part of the season.

Reflection

Working inside a regulated QC/QA system reinforced how important consistency and documentation are. Small process improvements often matter more than new technology.

Built slowly. Improved continuously.
